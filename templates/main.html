Productivity Measurements

‘Not everything that can be counted … counts’ - Albert Einstein

Although we offer insights to individual performance in Standard Metrics, we believe they belong in the ‘Flawed Five’. (https://leaddev.com/reporting-metrics/flawed-five-engineering-productivity-metrics)

Our rationale:

We believe the true measure of team performance is the time it takes to reach a predetermined goal. (Abi Noda, 2019). Using this hypothesis we measure the time between a pull request being opened and closed, the number of pull requests being closed, the number of issues being closed and the time in which those issues are being closed. We are also prototyping how many unit tests fail in a build and how long it takes to fix those failures (Alperowitz et al., 2016).

As above, productivity is more importantly a measure of time rather than output. The ‘ Flawed Five’ can easily be manipulated whereas the metrics below cannot.

We also are in the early development stages of measuring SCRUM productivity, we measure our own below. This will look to build upon the research carried out by Cardozo et al. (2010) and determine whether the agile development and SCRUM meeting techniques are productive in your organisation.
